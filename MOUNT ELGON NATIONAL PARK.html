MOUNT ELGON NATIONAL PARK

Mount Elgon National Park is a protected area in eastern Uganda that covers an area of 1,110 square kilometers (430 square miles). The park is named after Mount Elgon, an extinct volcano that straddles the Uganda-Kenya border and is the main attraction of the park.

Mount Elgon is the fourth highest mountain in East Africa, with the highest peak, Wagagai, standing at 4,321 meters (14,177 feet) above sea level. The mountain is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including giant lobelias, heath vegetation, monkeys, hyrax, buffalo, and various bird species.

The park offers a range of activities for visitors, including hiking to the mountain peaks, nature walks, bird watching, rock climbing, and cultural tours to the nearby communities. The park is also home to a variety of hot springs, caves, and waterfalls that provide stunning views for visitors.

The local communities around Mount Elgon National Park, including the Bagisu and Sabiny, have their unique cultural practices and beliefs that are worth exploring. The Imbalu circumcision ceremony, a traditional rite of passage for young men, is one such ceremony that draws visitors to the area.

Mount Elgon National Park is a unique destination for nature and adventure lovers, offering a chance to explore the beauty of the mountain, the diverse wildlife, and the cultural heritage of the local communities..
