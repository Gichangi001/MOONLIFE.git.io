RUMA NATIONAL PARK

Ruma National Park is a wildlife reserve located in western Kenya, near the shores of Lake Victoria. The park covers an area of 120 square kilometers and is named after the Ruma River that flows through the reserve. It is the only remaining habitat of the endangered Roan antelope in Kenya.

The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including buffalo, giraffe, hyena, leopard, and several primate species. It is also home to over 400 bird species, making it a popular destination for birdwatchers. The park is known for its beautiful scenery, including rolling hills, rocky outcrops, and acacia woodlands.

Visitors to Ruma National Park can enjoy game drives, guided walks, and birdwatching tours. There are several campsites and lodges within the park, offering visitors a chance to experience the beauty of the African wilderness while enjoying the comforts of modern amenities. Ruma National Park is a relatively off-the-beaten-path destination in Kenya, making it a great choice for travelers who want to get away from the crowds and experience a more authentic safari experience.
